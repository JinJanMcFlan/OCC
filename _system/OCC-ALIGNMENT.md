# OCC Alignment — Identity and Principles

OCC means Overseer Command Center.

OCC is a standalone, IDE-scale AI orchestration app. It is not an extension and does not live inside VS Code. OCC can ship an installable VS Code connector plugin as one reach into the editor, but the product is the standalone app.

The app is the brain. Models are swappable drivers. The intelligence — routing, delegation, capability and pricing knowledge, budget rules — lives in OCC. Any model can sit in any role.

Routing is deterministic and capability-driven. Decisions are made by a classifier and rule layer over a knowledge base of what each agent is good at and what it costs — not by a large model rolling dice.

OCC is provider-agnostic. No single provider, model, framework, or protocol is required or locked in.

OCC is local-first where useful. Projects bind to real disk folders. Compute that can run locally, should.

OCC is a stable survival layer over changing AI tools, providers, pricing, and capability. As the landscape shifts, OCC's control layer remains constant while the drivers beneath it change.

OCC may incorporate locally hosted cognitive execution packages beneath its provider-agnostic control layer. These packages can make multi-model work more efficient, private, and controllable than ordinary text-based orchestration.

Do not make AGI claims, implementation promises, or provider-specific commitments in any OCC document, prompt, or public-facing material.
