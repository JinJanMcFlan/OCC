OCC Router Research Synthesis and Decision Package1. Founder BriefThe Overseer Command Center (OCC) router architecture must establish a stable, deterministic orchestration layer that sits above an inherently volatile landscape of artificial intelligence providers, models, and tools. The synthesis of research runs R1 through R5 provides a concrete, evidence-supported foundation for how OCC will interpret tasks, govern capabilities, and safely integrate execution outcomes. The router is now definitively understood to require a strict separation between understanding a task's absolute constraints and mathematically scoring the available capabilities. It necessitates a hybrid deterministic pipeline where hard restrictions—such as local privacy mandates, explicitly required tool access, and firm budget caps—eliminate entirely unsuitable models before any comparative evaluation occurs. Furthermore, the router requires a highly protected outcome-feedback loop that cleanly severs objective operational telemetry, such as API health and execution duration, from subjective semantic quality evaluations, such as how effectively a model reasoned through a complex logic puzzle.Within this architecture, the Research Engine serves a highly specific, epistemological function. It is a system for maintaining long-term research objectives, managing hypothesis registers, collecting contradictory evidence, and preparing decision-ready handoffs for MainOrch. The Research Engine is not, and must never become, a secondary router. It does not possess the authority to schedule work, assign computational capabilities, maintain its own cost ledger, or execute workflows independently. When the Research Engine requires computational labor to test a hypothesis, it must generate a standardized bounded task and submit it to the primary OCC Router for execution. This internal dogfooding approach ensures OCC maintains a single, uncorrupted work history, preventing architectural drift and neutralizing the risk of an unmonitored automation loop bankrupting the system.Following MainOrch review, several structural components can safely proceed into active implementation. The project can immediately instantiate the baseline JavaScript Object Notation (JSON) and in-memory storage scaffolding, as no permanent database engine has been ratified. The foundational data schemas representing versioned task facts, capability evidence ladders, and objective outcome records can be drafted safely. The hard-rule gating logic, which strictly filters out invalid options based on non-negotiable constraints, is sufficiently supported by the R3 audit to begin preliminary construction. Furthermore, the base user interface paradigms—specifically the separation of the command thread, queue views, and the foundational data structures required for Presentation Mode—can safely begin receiving real-time data from the initial static mock registries.Conversely, certain mechanisms cannot be locked into the architecture yet. OCC does not currently possess the empirical evidence required to formalize the exact mathematical scoring formula for the router. Advanced automated semantic quality updates—where the system automatically demotes a model's global routing eligibility based on a single failed prompt or the judgment of a secondary evaluator model—are explicitly deferred. Furthermore, no external workflow scheduling framework, distributed tracing protocol, or secondary routing engine is locked, as the current direction remains fully provider-neutral and bespoke to OCC's unique determinism requirements.The practical value of this completed research is the immediate establishment of a provider-neutral survival layer. As closed-source models deprecate, pricing models fluctuate, and new local-compute paradigms emerge, OCC’s control plane will remain undisturbed. By treating intelligence as a swappable commodity driven by a proprietary, deterministic set of routing rules, OCC protects the human operator from ecosystem volatility. This research ensures OCC does not rely on opaque generative models to make budget-critical routing decisions, guaranteeing that task dispatch remains explainable, repeatable, and strictly aligned with the operator's defined priorities.2. Evidence-Supported Router DirectionThe architectural direction for the OCC Router is stratified into accepted facts derived from the research audits, candidate patterns requiring empirical observation, and explicitly rejected concepts. This categorization prevents the implementation layer from treating speculative research hypotheses as established product requirements.Accepted Direction Supported by AuditsThe fundamental pipeline of the OCC Router is accepted as a deterministic progression, relying on structured data rather than open-ended text generation. The process initiates with a Classifier that extracts structured task facts from a natural language request, ensuring the system routes based on explicit, trackable parameters rather than generic, unhelpful labels like "coding" or "research". Routing decisions rely exclusively on a dynamically updated Capability Registry, an evidence-based ledger detailing the proven abilities, costs, and availability of all integrated models, local machines, and APIs.The routing logic is permanently bound to five locked, independent factors: Cost, Time/Urgency, User Presence, Model Fit, and Output Quality Tier. A highly critical accepted finding from the R1 audit is the absolute architectural separation of urgency and user presence. Urgency represents a decay function indicating how rapidly a task's output loses its utility over time, whereas user presence is a binary or probabilistic indicator of whether the human operator is actively monitoring the command center and available to provide approvals. A critical system maintenance task might be highly urgent while the user is absent, requiring autonomous execution within strict safety bounds, while an exploratory research task might be entirely non-urgent while the user is actively engaged, allowing for interactive review and deferred batch processing to optimize costs.Furthermore, the R4 audit solidifies the necessity of bifurcating outcome feedback into operational metrics and semantic updates. Operational facts—such as precise execution costs, measured network latency, verified tool functionality, and API rate limits—must update the Capability Registry automatically and instantly. If a cloud provider experiences a verifiable outage, OCC will automatically restrict that route. However, semantic updates regarding the qualitative intelligence of a response must never alter a capability's global routing eligibility without accumulated statistical evidence and deliberate human review. This deliberate friction prevents the system from overreacting to isolated failures, poorly constructed prompts, or the subjective hallucinations of secondary evaluator models.Candidate Design Patterns Awaiting TestingThe primary candidate pattern dominating the active architectural discussion is the "Split-Gate Interpretation" originally surfaced in the R3 research run. The baseline pipeline hypothetically places all hard-rule gates after weighted scoring. The R3 candidate pattern posits that these gates must be split into two distinct operational phases to maintain deterministic integrity. Pre-scoring gates would aggressively filter out capabilities that violate absolute task facts, removing models that lack required local file access, fail strict privacy thresholds, or natively lack the required context window. Post-scoring gates would subsequently act as a final, time-of-execution safety check, verifying that the estimated cost of the mathematically selected route has not exceeded the dynamic budget and halting the process if human approval is mandated. This interpretation is highly logical but remains a candidate pattern pending the explicit MainOrch ratification detailed later in this package.A secondary candidate pattern revolves around the precise event-sourcing mechanisms required to translate execution states into the Presentation Mode's cinematic graph interface. The R5 audit suggests that every directed acyclic graph relationship—such as a blocker, dependency, or parent-child task link—must be captured as an immutable event in the decision log at the exact time of creation. While mapping graph nodes directly to task states is an accepted requirement, the specific data serialization mechanism to achieve this without an external graph database dependency remains a candidate pattern awaiting rigorous architectural testing.Explicitly Rejected or Deferred IdeasOCC categorically rejects the integration of Large Language Models as primary decision-making engines for the routing infrastructure. Any system architecture that prompts a generative model to freely decide where computational work should be directed fundamentally violates OCC’s core mandate for explainable, deterministic, and budget-constrained orchestration. The system also explicitly rejects the wholesale adoption of external routing frameworks, heavily marketed libraries, or enterprise compliance wrappers as foundational dependencies, prioritizing the bespoke local-first approach.Automated semantic circuit breakers and global penalty formulas are deferred indefinitely. While OCC will meticulously track deterministic failures, utilizing a secondary AI model to subjectively grade a primary model's output and automatically punish the primary model's standing in the Capability Registry is deemed unacceptably dangerous for the initial build. Claims regarding Artificial General Intelligence, optimal mathematical guarantees of routing perfection, or arbitrary timeline-based minimum viable product language are strictly prohibited and scrubbed from all project planning.Items that Remain UnknownThe exact mathematical weighting formula utilized to balance and compare valid capabilities across the five locked factors remains unknown. While the variables are established, determining how a ten-percent increase in expected financial cost should mathematically offset a five-percent decrease in expected model fit cannot be solved theoretically. It requires empirical benchmarking against highly realistic, varied workloads. Furthermore, the optimal statistical threshold for when an accumulated series of human rejections should trigger a formal alert for an automatic downgrade of a capability's semantic fit remains unknown and requires later longitudinal observation within the dogfooding environment.3. Candidate Router Build PackageThis package defines the practical, technology-agnostic architectural schemas necessary to transition OCC from theoretical research into active implementation planning. It delineates the precise flow of data from task inception through execution, evaluation, and eventual visual presentation.The Task Facts OCC Must UnderstandBefore the OCC routing logic can evaluate a request, the initial Classifier stage must successfully extract and structure a comprehensive Task Fact record. This record is not permanently frozen upon creation; it is a continuously versioned artifact that updates dynamically as the user clarifies the request, as external dependencies resolve, or as task scope inevitably creeps. The system must cleanly identify the user goal, representing the overarching human objective, distinct from the expected result, which dictates the specific technical deliverable required, such as a compiled binary, a validated JSON payload, or a formatted markdown report.The system must capture the precise urgency decay curve separately from the binary state of user presence, allowing the scheduler to optimize cost by deferring non-urgent, autonomous tasks to off-peak batch processing windows. It must explicitly state the requested output quality tier to prevent the system from over-spending premium intelligence tokens on trivial background data formatting. The record must encapsulate cost sensitivity, determining whether the associated financial limits are strict, hard-stop caps or merely flexible, weighted guidelines.Crucially, the router needs to extract environmental and execution constraints. It must identify all required tools and files to ensure the assigned capability possesses the physical and network access required to succeed. It must strictly define permissions and privacy sensitivity, governing whether the task data is legally or ethically permitted to leave the local machine's memory boundary. The system must evaluate risk and reversibility, assessing whether a mid-task failure would cause permanent external damage requiring human intervention, or if it can be safely and automatically rolled back. Finally, OCC must track explicit task dependencies and mathematically evaluate whether the aggregate work size allows for safe, concurrent splitting into parallel sub-tasks.The Capability Facts OCC Must TrackThe Capability Registry serves as the router's active menu of available computational options, designed to retain a history of verifiable evidence rather than asserting absolute, unchangeable truths. For every integrated model, specialized tool, local execution script, or remote service API, OCC must persistently record its permitted work scope and verified access rights, explicitly detailing the external systems and local directories it is authorized to interact with. It must strictly log privacy and data-handling limits. Notably, if a cloud provider's privacy stance or training-data policy is entirely unknown or opaque, OCC must automatically treat that capability as unverified and mathematically ineligible for any task flagged with high privacy sensitivity, enforcing a default-safe security posture.Operational telemetry metrics must include the capability's expected execution speed, its hard context and input token limits, and a real-time, frequently polled availability and health status. Every semantic capability fact—such as a model's historical success rate for a specific architectural coding task—must be inextricably linked to an evidence source, categorizing whether the claim stems from official vendor marketing, direct local observation, or inferred secondary testing. This evidence must include a timestamp and a calculated confidence level. If a local GPU cluster drops offline, its availability status must immediately broadcast an update to the registry, instantly zeroing its routing weight to prevent cascading execution failures.The Minimum Outcome Facts OCC Must RecordImmediately following any execution attempt, regardless of its success or failure, the router must generate an immutable Outcome Record. This data structure captures the fundamental reality of the execution, beginning with whether the task technically completed, such as receiving a valid HTTP 200 response from an external API. It then records whether the resulting output met all defined deterministic checks, proving, for example, that generated code successfully compiled or that a requested data structure was parsed without syntax errors.The system objectively records the actual financial cost and the actual wall-clock execution time, continuously comparing these figures against the pre-routing estimates to iteratively refine the Cost Layer's future scheduling accuracy. It logs whether all required tools functioned as intended and if the system encountered operational blockers like unexpected rate limits or network timeouts. Finally, the Outcome Record documents whether the output ultimately required human intervention, manual correction, or outright rejection, explicitly noting the identity of the evaluator to prevent secondary AI models from secretly poisoning the evidence base.Candidate Work and Relationship RecordsTo maintain a truthful, auditable history that supports both the Research Engine and the Presentation Mode graph, OCC requires a unified set of relationship records. A core User Task represents the initial, overarching operator request, which may sequentially or concurrently spawn multiple Delegated Work Runs. Each specific run contains a deeply nested Capability Assignment, logging precisely which model or tool was chosen, accompanied by a decision-time evidence snapshot detailing exactly why it was selected over alternatives at that exact millisecond.If a delegated run cannot immediately proceed, a Dependency and Blocker Record physically links it to the prerequisite parent task. When a hard-rule gate requires human intervention, a Human Approval Record binds the paused delegated run to the user's pending action. The final, verified deliverable of any run is stored as a standalone Output Record, ensuring that subsequent downstream tasks can trace their exact inputs back to verified, completed prior work.The Required Task StatesThe enumeration of task states dictates both the router's internal processing behavior and the user interface's visual presentation. A newly generated task enters the Queued state immediately upon creation. It transitions to Running during active computational execution. If the task encounters a pre-execution safety gate or a mid-execution anomaly requiring human oversight, it enters the Waiting for User state. If a task cannot proceed because a designated prerequisite task remains incomplete, it is flagged as Waiting for Dependency.Tasks intentionally pushed to off-peak hours or batch processing APIs by the scheduler to optimize the cost factor are marked as Deferred. Tasks that encounter repeated, insurmountable failures or fundamentally missing critical information are transitioned to Blocked. An execution that terminates unexpectedly and cannot be safely rolled back is Failed, while a successfully verified and evaluated output marks the overarching task as Complete. Tasks intentionally terminated by human intervention or overriding system policies are permanently Cancelled.Approval, Dependency, and Failure BoundariesApproval boundaries act as absolute, non-negotiable hard stops within the routing pipeline. If a task is flagged by the Classifier with high risk, severe irreversibility, or sensitive external tool access, the router must hold execution and transition the task directly to the waiting state. Crucially, a lack of immediate human approval does not trigger automatic cancellation; it pauses the workflow indefinitely until explicitly resolved by the operator.Dependency boundaries exist to prevent cascading systemic failures. A child task cannot enter the weighted scoring phase, nor can it reserve computational capabilities, until its designated parent task has successfully produced a validated Output Record.Failure boundaries dictate strict fallback behavior to prevent data corruption. OCC must never automatically retry failed actions if the initial action involved external state changes—such as writing to an external database, deleting a local file, or sending an email—unless a verified, systematically planned rollback mechanism is explicitly known and documented prior to execution. For safe, read-only failures, such as an API timeout during a purely internal text summarization pass, the system may automatically retry using the next highest-scoring capability available on the routing table.Visibility in Routing Rationale and Queue/Graph ViewsThe decision logging system must export structured data that renders the internal routing rationale fully transparent to the human operator. The UI must be able to display exactly why a specific capability was selected, listing the precise points awarded across the five factors. Equally important, it must explicitly explain why alternate, seemingly viable options were rejected, specifically identifying which hard restriction, privacy constraint, or low weighted score disqualified them.The queue and visual graph views—particularly the cinematic Presentation Mode and the structured Mind Map View—must display absolute architectural truth. Visual nodes, representing tasks, and connecting edges, representing relationships, must read directly and exclusively from the underlying, immutable work history. If a task was delayed because of a dependency, the line connecting them must visibly indicate the blockage. If a human review is requested, the task node must physically translate to the Human Review Zone on the canvas, bound by a visible tether to its paused parent process. No fabricated links, inferred connections, or post-hoc relationship inventions are permitted under any circumstances.4. Router and Research Engine BoundaryA strict, non-negotiable architectural boundary exists between the Router and the Research Engine. This segregation is paramount to prevent OCC from unintentionally developing a secondary, unregulated execution system that operates outside the operator's financial and safety parameters.Exclusive OwnershipThe Router exclusively owns the execution, scheduling, and governance of all bounded work. It alone holds the systemic authority to assign specific capabilities to tasks, assess real-time capability health, estimate computational costs, apply hard safety and privacy rules, transition task states through the execution lifecycle, and record the objective post-execution outcomes.Conversely, the Research Engine exclusively owns the epistemology and long-term intelligence gathering of the system. It manages persistent research objectives, maintains detailed hypothesis registers, tracks systemic contradictions in capability performance, designs complex A/B experiments, and prepares structured, decision-ready handoffs for the founder. It is the analytical layer that evaluates whether a model's degraded performance on a specific task shape is a statistical anomaly or a permanent capability shift.Shared Records and Lifecycle FlowWhile their operational authorities are strictly segregated, both systems share read-access to the underlying, unified work history. The Research Engine continuously reads the Router's decision logs, outcome records, and cost metrics to perform its longitudinal evaluations.When the Research Engine determines that it needs to run an active experiment—for example, testing a newly released local model's ability to extract Python syntax against an established baseline—it does not execute the test itself. Instead, the research work becomes routed work. The Research Engine formulates a standard, structured Task Fact record and submits it directly to the Router's standard queue. The Router treats this research request exactly like a standard user task: it applies cost gates, mathematically assigns the requested capabilities, executes the comparative test, and records the outcome.Once the Outcome Record is generated by the Router, the raw research result becomes evidence. The Research Engine reads this Outcome Record, attaches it to the relevant tracking hypothesis, and applies a strict evidence-quality grading, marking it as a direct, empirical observation rather than an inferred marketing claim.Over time, as this evidence accumulates and successfully resolves historical contradictions, the aggregated body of evidence becomes a proposed MainOrch decision. The Research Engine bundles these findings into a formalized Decision Handoff, presenting the founder with clear, actionable data and a recommended system change.Only when MainOrch formally reviews and explicitly approves the handoff does a ratified decision exist. This ratified decision then systematically informs future implementation work, permanently altering the default operator weights in the Router's scoring policy or permanently downgrading a specific model's status within the active Capability Registry.5.
